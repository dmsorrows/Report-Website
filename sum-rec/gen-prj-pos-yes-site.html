Generic Project (Positive Findings with Site)

 

Option 1. Polygon(s)

 

SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS

 

At the request of McGeorge Contracting Co., Inc., Flat Earth Archeology, LLC conducted a Phase I cultural resources survey of approximately 22.1 ha (54.8 ac) for two proposed borrow pits in Faulkner County, Arkansas. McGeorge Contracting Co., Inc. provided Flat Earth Archeology with two KMZs detailing the perimeters of the proposed Project Area prior to the commencement of fieldwork. Personnel utilized these devices to maintain real-time location data on handheld electronic mapping devices, allowing the archeologists to accurately progress along each prescribed transect within the proposed Project Area. Flat Earth Archeology defined the west polygon as Area A and the east polygon as Area B. Flat Earth Archeology established 32 transects within Area A and 20 transects within Area B of the proposed Project Area. Shovel tests were investigated at a maximum of 20-m intervals along each transect during the pedestrian survey. Flat Earth Archeology personnel excavated a total of 448 shovel tests within the proposed Project Area, not including 178 shovel test locale that remained unexcavated. The circumstances that disallowed for the excavation of the unexcavated shovel tests included shovel test in roadway with impenetrable gravel deposition, shovel test on slope exceeding 20 percent grade, shovel test in lake, shovel test in fully excavated borrow pit area, and shovel test in inaccessible water compound area. All the soils from excavated shovel test s were screened through Â¼ inch hardware mesh.

The above-ground survey and subsurface survey produced positive results. Shovel Tests 2 and 3 of Transect B, Shovel Tests 1 and 2 of Transect C, and Shovel Test 1 of Transect D of Area B produced positive results. Site 3WA1582 is characterized by a lithic scatter occurring predominantly in the uppermost levels of the soil strata. Seventy-nine percent of the deposits occur at 0-20 cmbs and 21 percent of the deposits occur at 20-30 cmbs over a 1,915 sq. m area, suggesting the deposits at Site 3WA1582 are very sparse. All artifacts are non-diagnostic and confound efforts to establish a cultural affiliation at the site. The artifact assemblage is not sufficient to support a recommendation that the site is eligible for listing in the NRHP. In the opinion of Flat Earth Archeology, Site 3WA1582 does not meet the criteria for inclusion in the NRHP as per 36 CFR 60.4. No further archeological work is recommended.

 

In the event of an inadvertent discovery of human remains and/or burial furniture during subsequent development or modification of the Project Area, the proponent should follow the protocols outlined in Act 753 of 1991, as amended (Arkansas Grave Protection Act) and other applicable state and federal laws and regulations. If previously unrecorded buried cultural resources are encountered during project construction, all ground disturbing activities in this area should be halted and the site should be protected until cleared by the appropriate authorities.

 

There is a realistic limitation involved with standard survey field methodology. Shovel testing is most effective in finding certain types of sites, those with relatively high artifact densities, or those with abnormal soil development such as middens. Thin artifact scatters are often difficult to identify in areas where surface visibility is poor. Furthermore, deeply buried sites are difficult to identify using standard survey methodology. Flat Earth Archeology made a good faith effort to locate cultural resources in the Project Area, but this is not a guarantee that no other cultural resources are present.

 

Option 2. Corridor with Defined Boundary

 

SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS

 

At the request of GBMc & Associates, Flat Earth Archeology, LLC conducted a Phase I cultural resources survey of 3.3 ha (8.16 ac) in Pulaski County, Arkansas. GBMc & Associates provided Flat Earth Archeology with a map and KMZs detailing the perimeter of the Project Area prior to the commencement of fieldwork. Flat Earth Archeology utilized ArcMap 10.4 to establish one transect within the Project Area and created a polyline shapefile delineating the transect. Additionally, personnel established shovel test locales along the transect polyline shapefile at 20-m intervals. Shapefiles were created based upon the shovel test locale point data. Flat Earth Archeology converted these shapefiles to KMZs. The archeologist utilized these devices to maintain real-time location data on handheld electronic mapping devices, allowing the archeologist to accurately investigate each shovel test locale along the prescribed transect within the Project Area.

 

The transect for the Project Area extended approximately 6,012 m (19,723 ft). The archeologist excavated a total of 24 shovel tests within the Project Area, not including 193 shovel tests that remained unexcavated. The varying occurrences of the unexcavated shovel tests for this area included shovel test on impenetrable asphalt street, shovel test on 7th Street Bridge, etc. All the soils from excavated shovel tests were screened through Â¼ inch hardware mesh.

Option 3. Corridor without Defined Boundary

 

SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS

 

At the request of Miller-Newell Engineers, Inc., Flat Earth Archeology, LLC conducted a Phase I cultural resources survey of 3.6 km (2.2 mi) of proposed waterline improvements in Poinsett County, Arkansas. Miller-Newell Engineers, Inc. provided Flat Earth Archeology with maps detailing the centerlines of the proposed waterline improvements comprising the Project Area prior to the commencement of fieldwork. Flat Earth Archeology utilized ArcMap 10.4 to georeference the maps provided by Miller-Newell Engineers, Inc. and created polyline shapefiles along the proposed waterline improvement centerlines. These data were converted to KMZ files to facilitate the use on handheld electronic mapping devices. The archeologist utilized this device to maintain real-time location data, allowing the archeologist to accurately progress along each prescribed transect within the Project Area.

 

Flat Earth Archeology divided the Project Area into nine linear transects, numbered 1 through 9. Flat Earth Archeology established a 9.1 m (30 ft) wide corridor along the linear transects to adequately identify cultural resources within and proximal to the polylines detailing the centerlines of the proposed waterline improvements comprising the Project Area. Shovel tests were investigated at a maximum of 20-m intervals along each transect during the pedestrian survey. Flat Earth Archeology personnel excavated a total of 89 shovel tests within the Project Area, not including five shovel tests that remained unexcavated. The circumstances that disallowed for the excavation of the unexcavated shovel tests included shovel test in area of severe mechanical disturbance and shovel test in active drainage. All the soils from excavated shovel tests were screened through Â¼ inch hardware mesh.
