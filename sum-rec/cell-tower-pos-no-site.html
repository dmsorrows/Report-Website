Cell Tower (Positive Findings without Site)

 

Option 1. Monopole

 

SUMMARY AND RECOMMENDATIONS

 

At the request of EnviroWest, LLC., Flat Earth Archeology, LLC conducted a Phase I cultural resources survey for the proposed 21-ft CRAN_ROAR_DTLRS_389 monopole telecommunications tower site in Pulaski County, Arkansas. The UTM coordinate for the center of the proposed tower is Zone 15S, 561842mE, 3846247mN. The Project Area is situated in the southeast quarter of Section 31 of Township 2 North, Range 13 West.

 

The small area of the proposed metal pole footprint defining the Project Area is less than one square meter and is completely covered by concrete. Therefore, the archeologist excavated one shovel test locale as proximal as possible to the Project Area. The archeological fieldwork was conducted by archeologist Devin Sorrows, RPA. Field work was conducted on October 28, 2021. All the soils from the shovel test locale were screened through ¼-inch woven hardware cloth. A pedestrian survey of the entire project was conducted in addition to the shovel test to examine the ground surface for cultural materials. The above-ground survey and subsurface survey produced positive results. Personnel discovered an occurrence of modern cultural materials in the proposed Project Area. Each occurrence of modern cultural materials did not meet the criteria of an archeological site as defined by Davis, ed. (1982, amended 2010:B-8). Therefore, the occurrence was not recorded as an archeological site.

There is a realistic limitation involved with standard survey field methodology. Shovel testing is most effective in finding certain types of sites, those with relatively high artifact densities, or those with abnormal soil development such as middens. Thin artifact scatters are often difficult to identify in areas where surface visibility is poor. Furthermore, deeply buried sites are difficult to identify using standard survey methodology. Flat Earth Archeology made a good faith effort to locate cultural resources in the Project Area, but this is not a guarantee that no other cultural resources are present.
