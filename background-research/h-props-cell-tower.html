Option 1. There are Historic Properties within Visual APE

 

For ABSTRACT

 

Personnel conducted a record check of the Arkansas Historic Preservation Program (AHPP) geographic information system (GIS) National Register and Survey Database and the AMASDA database. The Visual Area of Potential Effect (APE) for the proposed 146 ft equipment replacement is a ½-mile radius pursuant to the 2004 Nationwide Programmatic Agreement for Review of Effects on Historic Properties for Certain Undertakings Approved by the Federal Communications Commission. According to the AHPP and AMASDA databases, there is one historic property as defined by 36 CFR 800.16(l)(1) within the ½-mile Visual APE. The historic property, Site 3PU0773 (Rocky Mountain Cemetery), was revisited and photographed. The scope of work for the proposed undertaking includes the replacement of existing electrical equipment with new electrical equipment on the existing elevated water storage tank. The proposed equipment replacements will not exhibit a sufficient size or contrast to diminish any of the aspects of integrity of Site 3PU0773 (Rocky Mountain Cemetery) that qualify the historic property for inclusion in the NRHP. Additionally, hardwood and softwood trees between the historic property and Project Area Location act as a natural vegetative screen from the proposed equipment replacement. The presence of these visual obstructions preserves the historic property's aspects of integrity. Therefore, Flat Earth Archeology recommends a finding of no adverse effect as per 36 CFR 800.5 (b) for the ½-mile Visual APE on Site 3PU0773.
