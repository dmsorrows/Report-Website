Historic Properties Options for Generic Projects (Templates for each Chapter throughout Report)

 

Option 1. There are Historic Properties In or Near Project Area

 

For ABSTRACT

 

A review of the Arkansas Historic Preservation Program (AHPP) geographic information system (GIS) National Register and Survey Database and the AMASDA database managed by the Arkansas Archeological Survey (ARAS) indicated there is one historic property, as defined by 36 CFR 800.16(l)(1), within the Project Area. The proposed undertaking extends approximately 435.6 m (1,429 ft) north into the southwestern portion of Boyle Park Historic District and includes the development of a pedestrian trail. The proposed pedestrian trail is expected to accord in likeness and appearance with other pedestrian trails featured throughout the historic district. Moreover, the proposed undertaking will comport with the historical purpose of the park, as detailed in the warranty deed authorizing the transfer of title to the land explicitly stipulating that the park be used for “recreational purposes” only. It is very unlikely the proposed trail will introduce visual, atmospheric or audible elements that diminish the integrity of the property’s significant historic features. The proposed trail and its associated construction will not adversely affect this property’s aspects of integrity. Therefore, Flat Earth Archeology recommends a finding of no adverse effect pursuant to 36 CFR 800.5 (b) for the proposed undertaking. Flat Earth Archeology recommends no further archeological work for the Project Area.

It is very unlikely the proposed trail will introduce visual, atmospheric or audible elements that diminish the integrity of the property’s significant historic features. The proposed trail and its associated construction will not adversely affect this property’s aspects of integrity. Therefore, Flat Earth Archeology recommends a finding of no adverse effect as per 36 CFR 800.5 (b) for the proposed undertaking.

Option 2. There are No Historic Properties In or Near Project Area

 

For ABSTRACT

 

A review of the Arkansas Historic Preservation Program (AHPP) geographic information system (GIS) National Register and Survey Database and the Automated Management of Archeological Site Data in Arkansas (AMASDA) database managed by the Arkansas Archeological Survey (ARAS) indicated there are no historic properties, as defined by 36 CFR 800.16(l)(1), within or proximal to the Project Area.

 

Based on the results of the background research and survey, Flat Earth Archeology recommends that the proposed undertaking meets the criteria for a finding of No Historic Properties Affected pursuant to 36 CFR 800.4(d)(1). Flat Earth Archeology recommends no further archeological work for the Project Area.
