Improvements on USGS Maps and/or Aerial Imagery (Background Research Chapter Only)

 

Option 1. There are Improvements In or Near Project Area

 

Survey maps produced throughout the nineteenth and twentieth centuries by the USGS were reviewed for historical improvements within or proximal to the Project Area. The 1891 Little Rock, AR Topographic Quadrangle Map and 1893 (1918 edition) Camp Pike, AR Topographic Quadrangle Map detail a roadway paralleling the western boundary of the Project Area (Figures 23 and 23) (USGS 2022; USGS 2022a). The 1935 Sweet Home, AR Topographic Quadrangle Map details a roadway paralleling the western boundary of the Project Area and two structures associated with the Thibault family in the northwestern and southwestern portions of the Project Area. A third Thibault associated structure is also present between the two western extents of the Project Area on this map (Figure 23) (USGS 2022b).

 

Aerial imagery acquired throughout the middle to late twentieth century by the USDA was also reviewed for historical improvements within or proximal to the Project Area. December 1943 Aerial Imagery details the three structures associated with the Thibault family as illustrated in the 1935 Sweet Home, AR Topographic Quadrangle Map. Additionally, farmland dominates the Project Area (Figure 34). By April 1960, the two northernmost structures associated with the Thibault family farm are no longer extant. Woodland begins to encroach upon the locale of the Thibault family home and area north of the home (Figure 23). While a majority of the farmland remains in use throughout the Project Area into the late twentieth century, the areas previously encroached upon by the woodland become dominated by it (Figures 23 and 23). February 1984 Aerial Imagery details the Thibault house site prior to its burning in 1989.

Option 2. There are No Improvements In or Near Project Area

 

Survey maps and aerial imagery produced throughout the twentieth century by the USGS and USDA were reviewed for historical improvements within or proximal to the Project Area. October 1949 Aerial Imagery and 1961 (1963 edition) Conway, AR Topographic Quadrangle Map detail no historical improvements within or proximal to the Project Area (USGS 2022) (Figures 22 and 23).
