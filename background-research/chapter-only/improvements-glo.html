First Land Patent Records and Historical Improvements

 

The First Land Patents records and the General Land Office maps were also consulted for information regarding the history of land ownership of the Project Area. The Bureau of Land Management’s First Land Patent records contain many of the names of the initial legal landowner for each section of land. These records generally contain other information regarding the first legal landowners such as how the land was obtained (i.e. homestead, cash entry, scrip warrant, etc.), the acreage obtained in the patent, the legal description of the land, and the date of the patent issuance. Moreover, General Land Office Maps usually show areas with historical development, often depicting improvements such as agricultural fields, roads, or structures, along with names of landowners. The General Land Office (GLO) original survey map for Township 1 North, Range 11 West, approved in 1819, details no historical improvements within or proximal to the Project Area (Figure 19) (GLO 2022). The GLO dependent resurvey map or Township 1 North, Range 11 West, approved in 1857, details an agricultural field encompassing a majority of the Project Area with a homestead situated in the southwestern extent of the Project Area (Figure 19) (GLO 2022a). The GLO dependent resurvey map also lists “Mr. Thibault” as the owner of these historical improvements.

 

GLO land patent records show that on April 15, 1837 Bazil Hudgins acquired the southeast quarter of the southeast quarter of Section 21 in Township 1 North, Range 11 West via Sale-Cash Entry authorized by the Act of Congress of April 24th, 1820 (GLO 2022b). GLO land patent records show that on April 15, 1837 Richard Graves acquired the west half of the southwest quarter of Section 22 in Township 1 North, Range 11 West via Sale-Cash Entry authorized by the Act of Congress of April 24th, 1820 (GLO 2022c). GLO land patent records show that on October 11, 1839 Isariah Zachary acquired the east half of the southeast quarter of Section 28 in Township 1 North, Range 11 West via Sale-Cash Entry authorized by the Act of Congress of April 24th, 1820 (GLO 2022d).
