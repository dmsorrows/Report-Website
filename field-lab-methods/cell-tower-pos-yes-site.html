Option 1. Monopole

 

LABORATORY METHODOLOGY

 

Artifacts recovered during this project were assigned Field Serial Numbers (FSN) denoting the minimum spatial provenience. The FSNs were assigned sequentially by 10-cm level in shovel tests. The FSN was determined by when the artifacts were recorded into the FSN log by the individual archeologists in the field. Surface finds received an FSN. Upon delivery of artifacts to the lab, Flat Earth Archeology personnel confirmed the contents of each artifact bag and logged it on the Lab Intake Ledger Form. Artifacts were verified against the FSN logs from the field.

 

Prior to analysis, the artifacts were processed according to established best practices for each material type. New lab tags were created to capture provenience written on any field bags and or tags and placed on the drying screens with the contents of each bag. Project, spatial, site, and general notes were transcribed onto the new archival quality tags. If appropriate, artifacts were gently washed in warm water with soft brushes and placed on screen racks to air dry. Alternative methods of cleaning involved gentle dry brushing with a soft bristled brush. Processing of artifacts was conducted either by Lab Manager Laura Fuentes, RPA or under their direct supervision. The artifacts were roughly sorted during processing based on material types such as stone, glass, ceramic, pottery, or metal. Once fully processed and dried, artifacts were transferred with their Lab Bag Tags to archival quality white block bags until they could be analyzed. In the event that materials were determined to be naturally occurring instead of cultural materials, they were noted as such in the FSN log and deaccessioned using an appropriate method.

 

All artifacts were sorted based on their primary material composition into basic Laboratory Serial Number (LSN) classes: Unmodified Raw Material, Stone, Fauna and Flora, Pottery, Ceramic, Glass, Metal, Other & Unknown. Pre and Post Contact artifacts were further defined based on observable characteristics, further refining the basic material classification (i.e.: Burlington Chert, Novaculite, Shell Tempered, Stoneware, Brown Glass...). This second category is defined as the first level of the Artifact Serial Number (ASN 1). The third division (ASN 2) describes use, function, category, or diagnostic form (i.e.: Tertiary Flake, Bowl Sherd, Cut Nail, Beer Can Pull Tab...). The fourth and final division in artifact sorting (ASN 3) is used to describe specific diagnostic decoration styles, treatments, shapes, or sizes (i.e.: Burnished, Heat Modified, Transferware, Embossed, 16-D (1'' nail size)).

 

This model of artifact analysis is built as an adaptation of both DELOS: A Computerized Artifact Inventory and Analysis System (Cande and Barnes 1992) and the Digital Archaeological Archive of Comparative Slavery's Cataloging Manual (DAACS 2018). Cande's work provided the base model for category divisions, moving from general material types to more specific observable characteristics and types. Specific definitions and type restrictions were used as a guideline for identifying artifact types or subsets. The Digital Archaeological Archive of Comparative Slavery's (DAACS) was referenced to help delineate specific categories or characteristic definitions and parameters.

 

The artifact catalog includes the complete catalog number for each artifact, comprised of the accession number assigned by the Arkansas Archeological Survey (ARAS), the FSN, LSN, ASN, ASN 1, and ASN 2 and ASN 3 as appropriate. Lab personnel counted, weighed, and described all unique or diagnostic characteristics of each artifact. A representative sample of artifact types and frequency were photographed using a color photo scale utilizing the metric scale. Additionally, diagnostic or unique artifacts were selected to be photographed. All artifacts were prepared for permanent curation in accordance with the most recently updated standards of the University of Arkansas Collections Facility in Fayetteville, Arkansas (University of Arkansas Collections Facility 2018).
